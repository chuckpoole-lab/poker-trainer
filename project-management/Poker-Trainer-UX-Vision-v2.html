Poker Trainer — UX Vision

Two Paths, One App · v2 · April 5, 2026 · For review: Chris & Chuck

1. The Core Insight

Not every player wants the same thing. A casual bar player who shows up Tuesday nights wants to have fun and get a little better. A grinder studying ranges and plugging leaks wants focused, efficient training. The app needs to serve both without forcing one through the other's experience.

The Daily Hands challenge is the bridge. It's the Wordle mechanic: same 5 hands for everyone, every day. Streaks, IQ scores, leaderboards. That's the daily reason to open the app regardless of which path you're on.

2. Proposed Color Direction: "Wordle for Poker"

The current dark charcoal theme feels cold and intimidating. We're shifting to a bright, warm palette inspired by Wordle's clean aesthetic combined with poker table greens and golds. The goal: an app that feels inviting enough to open every day.

7. Scoring Philosophy: Best Answers & Acceptable Plays

Poker is a game of making decisions with incomplete information. While some decisions are clearly right or wrong, many spots have a best answer and an acceptable answer. The app should reflect this reality.

Current behavior: The scoring system already supports three tiers: CORRECT (1.0 pts), ACCEPTABLE (0.7 pts), and LEAK (0.0 pts). But the feedback doesn't communicate this well.

Proposed feedback language:

ScoreCurrent MessageProposed Message
Correct"Correct!""That's the play." + brief explanation why
Acceptable"Acceptable""I see where you're coming from — that's an acceptable play. But here's the stronger move and why..." + explanation of the edge
Leak"Leak Found""Not this time. Here's why, and what to look for in this spot..." + clear explanation

This approach respects the player's reasoning while teaching them to find the edge. It's coaching, not quizzing.

Scenario Validation Rules (Critical)

Every dynamically generated hand must pass validation before being presented to the player. The logic must be contextual to position. Example of a current flaw: "Everyone folds to you in UTG" makes no sense because UTG is first to act — no one has had the chance to fold yet.

Proposed validation rules the generator must enforce:

  • UTG/UTG+1: Can only see "unopened" scenarios (they are first to act). Never "folds to you" or "facing open."
  • Middle positions (MP, LJ, HJ): Can see "unopened" (everyone before them folded) or "facing open" (earlier position raised).
  • Late positions (CO, BTN): Can see "unopened," "facing open" from any earlier position, or "facing multiple opens."
  • Blinds (SB, BB): Can see "unopened" (rare — folded to SB), "facing open" from any position, or "facing raise + callers."
  • Stack depth logic: At 10bb, there is no "raise" — it's jam or fold. At 15bb, raise is possible but jam is often correct. At 20bb+, full raise/fold/call logic applies.
  • Action consistency: The available actions must match the scenario. Don't offer "Call" when no one has bet. Don't offer "Raise" at 10bb.
  • Reject ambiguous hands: If a hand has no clear best answer backed by the GTO range tables, reject it and generate a new one.
Build approach: Create a validation layer that tests every generated hand against these rules. The generator creates a candidate hand, the validator checks it. If it fails, reject and regenerate. Log rejections for debugging. This ensures every hand the player sees makes perfect sense.

9. Data-Driven Player Profile (Serious Players)

What we track:

  • Every hand decision: position, stack depth, action taken, correct action, score (correct/acceptable/leak)
  • Accuracy by position (UTG through BB)
  • Accuracy by stack depth (10bb, 15bb, 20bb, 30bb)
  • Accuracy by scenario type (unopened, facing open, facing 3-bet)
  • Accuracy by leak category (9 categories)
  • Trends over time (7-day, 30-day)

What we surface:

  • Play Style Summary: "Tight-passive from early position, loose-aggressive on the button" — derived from decision patterns
  • Leak Map: Visual grid showing strong vs. weak spots by position × stack depth
  • Top 3 Leaks: Weakest categories with specific drill recommendations. "Your early position discipline is at 58% — drill 15 hands from UTG."
  • Improvement Trend: "Your accuracy improved from 62% to 74% this month"
  • Training Recommendations: "Based on your data, here's what to work on today"

What casual players DON'T see:

No homework. No guilt. Just the daily challenge, the streak, and gentle encouragement. Casual players don't need a leak analysis telling them they're bad at blind defense. They're here to have fun.

Moving profile data to Supabase

Currently all progress data lives in the browser's localStorage. This means switching browsers or clearing cache loses everything. Moving to Supabase means:

  • Benefit: Data persists across devices. Play on phone, check progress on desktop.
  • Benefit: We can build real analytics and player profiles from the data.
  • Benefit: Enables cross-player comparisons and league-level stats.
  • Impact: Requires new Supabase tables for drill history, hand-level decisions, and computed stats. RLS policies for privacy. Migration path for existing localStorage data.
  • Impact: Guests would continue using localStorage. Only registered users get cloud persistence.
  • Effort: Medium — 2-3 dev sessions to build tables, migrate, and wire up.

10. Mode Switching & Guest Behavior

Casual → Serious

  • "Try Training Mode" bubble on the casual home screen lets them preview the training hub
  • They can explore freely, but next visit they revert to Play mode
  • Permanent switch available via a toggle in Settings ("Player Mode: Casual / Serious")
  • After milestones (e.g., 10 daily sessions, 5-day streak), prompt them: "Ready to level up? Switch to Training Mode."

Serious → Casual

  • Same Settings toggle. No judgment.

Guest behavior

  • See the welcome screen ("How do you play poker?") every time they visit
  • Exception: if we recognize the device fingerprint (we already track this), remember their choice
  • Guest data stays in localStorage. Prompt to sign in to save progress.
